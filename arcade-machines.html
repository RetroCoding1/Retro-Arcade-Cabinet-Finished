Arcade Machines

First off, what the heck even is an arcade machine? In wikipedia, it says that An arcade game or coin-op game is a coin-operated entertainment machine typically installed in public businesses such as restaurants, bars and amusement arcades. Most arcade games are presented as primarily games of skill (examples: Pac-Man, Street Fighter, Donkey Kong) and include arcade video games, pinball machines, electro-mechanical games, redemption games or merchandisers.
Unfortunately, I was born a little too late to experience the greatness of getting to go to the arcade with friends. Now that more modern and advanced games are available on consoles and PC, coin-operated games are obsolete.

The golden age of arcade machines was during the late 1970s through the 1980s, which was during my parent's teen years.

In the past I have built an small-scale arcade machine and a game to be run on said machine. As a product I could try porting some of my homemade games coded in Scratch (such as Rebellion's End) and put them onto the machine.
